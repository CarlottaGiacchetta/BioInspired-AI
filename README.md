# Bio-Inspired Artificial Intelligence - Laboratory Exercises

Welcome to the repository for the **Bio-Inspired Artificial Intelligence** course, taught by Prof. Giovanni Iacca at the University of Trento. These exercises explore key concepts of bio-inspired artificial intelligence, including evolutionary algorithms, multi-objective optimization, neuro-evolution, and co-evolutionary dynamics. Each lab provides a mix of theory and practice, leveraging Python libraries like `inspyred` and `deap`.

## Course Objective

The course aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of bio-inspired algorithms and their applications, fostering a practical and experimental approach. Through these labs, students will learn how to implement innovative algorithms to solve complex problems in various contexts.

---

## Laboratory Structure

### **Lab 1: Introduction to Evolutionary Algorithms**
- **Objectives:**
  - Analyze the effect of mutations in continuous optimization problems.
  - Study the "sphere" fitness function.
- **Applications:**
  - Investigate the impact of dimensionality and mutation magnitude on evolutionary adaptation.

### **Lab 2: Crossover and Selection Pressure**
- **Objectives:**
  - Explore the role of crossover and selection pressure.
  - Optimize common benchmarks like the Rastrigin function.
- **Applications:**
  - Parametric analysis of the effectiveness of evolutionary algorithms.

### **Lab 3: Evolution Strategies (ES)**
- **Objectives:**
  - Introduction to Evolution Strategies (ES) and CMA-ES.
  - Self-adaptation of parameters in continuous optimization problems.
- **Applications:**
  - Solve benchmarks like the Rosenbrock function.

### **Lab 4: Multi-Objective Optimization**
- **Objectives:**
  - Use NSGA-II to find Pareto fronts.
  - Solve multi-objective problems like Kursawe and DTLZ7.
- **Applications:**
  - Trade-off analysis for conflicting objectives.

### **Lab 5: Constraint Handling**
- **Objectives:**
  - Implement constraint-handling techniques.
  - Solve complex problems like multi-disk brake design.
- **Applications:**
  - Use penalties and constraints to find optimal solutions.

### **Lab 7: Ant Colony Optimization**
- **Objectives:**
  - Apply Ant Colony Optimization to discrete problems.
  - Solve classical problems like the Traveling Salesman Problem.
- **Applications:**
  - Compare evolutionary approaches to Ant Colony Systems.

### **Lab 8: Neuro-Evolution**
- **Objectives:**
  - Evolve weights and topologies of neural networks using NEAT.
  - Solve static and dynamic problems like "Xor" and "Temporal Xor."
- **Applications:**
  - Use recurrent neural networks for solving complex problems.

### **Lab 9: Evolutionary Robotics**
- **Objectives:**
  - Experiment with a maze navigation task using simulated robots.
  - Evolve neural network controllers to guide robots to a target.
- **Applications:**
  - Explore evolutionary strategies for dynamic and challenging environments.

### **Lab 10: Competitive Co-Evolution**
- **Objectives:**
  - Develop prey-predator dynamics using co-evolution.
  - Evolve Sorting Networks through competition between hosts and parasites.
- **Applications:**
  - Analyze co-evolutionary algorithms and implement them in practical problems.
